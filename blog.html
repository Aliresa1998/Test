Royal Theatre

Amy B Says: The Theatre Royal is an absolutely stunning building on Grey Street. It hosts high quality performances, with many musicals and plays coming direct from their runs in London's West End. Many shows have a matinee performance at 2 or 2.30pm which are great for the whole family to visit and enjoy. Tips: Your under 16s qualify for a child ticket and, for certain shows, under 2s can enter at a very discounted rate as "Babes in Arms". There is an accessible toilet in the stalls basement that has baby changing facilities. Matinee performances usually run on Thursday, Friday and Saturday, but this can vary. It is best to book tickets in advance via the theatre website. Plan to stay: 2-3 hours

Millennium Bridge

Amy B Says: No trip to Newcastle would be complete without a trip to the Quayside to see the River Tyne, bordered by Newcastle Upon Tyne on the northern bank, and Gateshead on the southern bank. There are some very impressive bridges, including the famous Tyne Bridge, and the pedestrian Gateshead Millennium Bridge. A lot of the routes from the city centre to the river are very steep or stepped. I recommend using a road called "Side" which is still steep but navigable with the pushchair. It is worth a walk across Millennium Bridge. If you are lucky, you will see it lifting to let larger boats pass underneath. Just beware the wobbles on windy days! If you visit on a Sunday, there is a good arts and crafts market along the Quayside with some tasty produce stalls too. Plan to stay: 1-2 hours

Angel Of The North

Travelers Says: It is worth stopping here for a closer look as it is easily accessible just off the A1 with a small car park so if you are travellng up the A1 and want a short break you may as well stop here and stretch your legs. It’s something to see if you’re ever up this way, and ever want a photo with something so spectacular and so at the heart of the north, that if you come here, it should be a quick stop on your way.
